Dairy Base

The core ingredients usually include milk and cream, which provide the rich, creamy texture.

Sweeteners

Sugar is the primary sweetening agent, though alternatives like honey, corn syrup, or artificial sweeteners can be used.

Flavorings

These can range from natural flavors like vanilla, chocolate, and strawberry to more complex and exotic ingredients like matcha, lavender, or fruit purees.

Additives

Stabilizers and emulsifiers are often added to improve texture and shelf-life. Common additives include guar gum, carrageenan, and lecithin.

Impact on Texture and Flavor

The fat content from the cream helps to stabilize the mixture and create a smooth, creamy mouthfeel. It also carries flavors well, enhancing the overall taste experience. The proteins in milk help to stabilize air bubbles during churning, contributing to the lightness and smoothness of the ice cream.

Milk and Cream

The dairy base is fundamental in ice cream, providing the creamy texture and rich flavor. Milk contributes water, proteins, and lactose, while cream adds fat, which is crucial for a smooth and creamy texture
